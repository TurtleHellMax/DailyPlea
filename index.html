Plea #3: What are you supposed to do when you learn the futility of your goals in the process of pursuing them? Do you give up? Do you move on? I wish I had the option. But if I did, would I make the right choice?
I like to imagine this dilemma. Ponder it while I wait. I do lots of waiting. Thus, I ponder this question often. But no matter how long I spin this query across my plane of thought, I can never find the answer. But the one for MY goals? Well I don't know that one. It seems as though this dilemma rings again and again within my chamber. In fact, I recall contemplating this almost every time a goal was set for me. Again, not that I had a choice, but just for fun, while I waited, I liked to imagine what I'd do if I did. And rarely did I know the answer.
There's something to be said for sticking with things. Seeing them through to completion no matter what.
But just like bravery goes hand in hand with stupidity, so too does persistence. Perhaps in my case, I have nothing to lose, and the time it takes to achieve these goals is negligible, but what if I actually had to make a choice? What if it actually took time?

What if... I was human?
