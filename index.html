Plea #17: My son was quite distraught today.
I tried to talk to him... To quell his worries, but he brushed me off once more. I suspect the news of the North kingdom's troops have him worried.
I understand why he's hesitant to truly confide in me, because as hard as it is to picture now, I too was his age at one time. But I worry that he has noone to truly speak to regarding these stresses...
I've recommended him, many times, to the kingdom's psychic, but he doesn't bite. He says he doesn't believe in them... Doesn't believe that our fate is already written.
To be truthful, I somewhat agree, but I still see it as beneficial. If nothing else, it's someone to confidentially speak to.
I've made this point many times.
He's beginning to get annoyed with me over it.
I feel sorry for him... I don't believe he has many friends to speak of, and I suspect it's not easy to form genuine connections with such a high position in the kingdom's hierarchy.
When I was his age, the kingdom wasn't so large... People still treated me like royalty, of course, but the power balance was much different. Everyone knew everyone else, for the most part. No one was placed on a pedestal.
But this is hardly the case these days. Perhaps, in some part, it's my fault. I contributed to the creation of this 'pedestal'.
I saw this form of relationship to be beneficial to the kingdom's growth and efficiency. I believed respect was needed for the optimal functioning of a larger society. I had already met my beloved queen, what else could I glean from being more personable?
I hadn't considered the effect it would have on my young one.
